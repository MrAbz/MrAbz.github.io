“The development of expertise is a long process, during which theoretical, practical and metacognitive elements of expert knowledge are integrated into a coherent whole.”

With the aim of developing my skill-sets I did my Summer Internship from Doordarshan Kendra, Lucknow. The curriculum of the internship comprised of Four modules: TV Studio, Editing, Terrestrial Platforms and Satellite Communication respectively. The initial lecture introduced us to the Lucknow Centre of Doordarshan.

The Module-1 comprised of base-band signals, TV camera, TV audio, Vision Mixing, TV Lighting and TV recordings. We learned about the various signals involved in TV broadcasting and their frequency ranges, the TV camera and the parameters affecting the recording system. The lectures were followed by visits to the DDK Studio and the OB Van.

The Module-2 involved the editing section. After the audio and video are recorded they are edited accordingly to get the best output. This can be done through the linear and the non-linear editing. Linear editing is becoming obsolete as it requires too much memory space. On the contrary non-linear editing makes use of computer software that reduces the memory usage. The lectures on this module were followed by a visit to the ENG department.

The Module-3 detailed us about the Terrestrial Platforms. We were briefed about the various transmitters and their working. This was followed by a visit to the DDK Transmitter Station.

The Module-4 dealt with the essentials of the Satellite Communication. A detailed study of satellites, their bands and paths was done. The look angle and link budget were computed. This was followed by a visit to the DDK Earth Station. Conclusively, this summer internship helped me to understand the theoretical and practical aspects of TV Broadcasting System. It was a great learning experience.
